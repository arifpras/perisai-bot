# Indonesia's Recent Developments (2024-2025)
**Source:** SEC Form 18-K/A Filings (July 2025, October 2025)
**Last Updated:** January 2, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Indonesia continues its economic recovery with a focus on inclusive and sustainable growth. Under the new administration of President Prabowo Subianto (since October 20, 2024), the country is pursuing an ambitious "Asta Cita" (Eight Aspirations) strategy emphasizing food security, energy independence, infrastructure development, and human capital investment.

---

## Political & Administrative Changes (2024-2025)

### Presidential Elections & New Administration
- **General Election Date:** February 14, 2024
- **New President:** Prabowo Subianto (sworn in October 20, 2024)
- **Vice President:** Gibran Rakabuming Raka
- **Term:** Until 2029

### Asta Cita Strategy (Eight Aspirations)
President Prabowo's comprehensive development strategy includes:

1. **Strengthening Pancasila, Democracy, and Human Rights**
2. **National Self-Reliance:** Food, energy, water, Islamic economy, digital economy, green economy, blue economy
3. **Infrastructure Development & Quality Employment**
4. **Improved Welfare & Poverty Reduction**
5. **Cultural & Mental Revolution**
6. **Environmental & Climate Resilience**
7. **Political, Legal & Security Stability**
8. **Public Service Transformation**

### Cabinet Restructuring
- Cabinet Secretariat dissolved; functions integrated into ministries
- Ministry of Environment split into:
  - Ministry of Environment (2025 budget: Rp1,079.8B)
  - Ministry of Forestry (2025 budget: Rp5,158.5B)

### 2025 Stimulus Package (December 2024)
Total allocation: **Rp38.6 trillion** (completed by February 2025 with Rp33.0T disbursed)

**Components:**
- Rice assistance: 10 kg/month to 16 million recipients
- Electricity discount: 50% for 2,200 VA consumers
- EV incentives: Electric & hybrid vehicle support
- Labor-intensive financing: Support for manufacturing

### Free Nutritious Meal Program (Launched January 6, 2025)

- **Additional budget:** Rp100 trillion for scale-up

### VAT Changes (Effective January 1, 2025)
- **Luxury goods:** Increased from 11% to 12%
- **Essential goods:** Remain at 0% exemption (rice, meat, fish, eggs, vegetables, etc.)
- **Standard goods:** Remain at 11%

## Infrastructure Development

### National Strategic Projects (PSNs)
- **Completed (2016-December 2024):** 229 PSNs
- **Total investment value:** Rp1,993 trillion
- **Funding model:** 40% public sector, 60% private sector

**Priority Areas:**
1. **Transportation:** Toll roads, rail, airports, ports
2. **Connectivity:** Broadband expansion (Palapa Ring)
3. **Energy:** Renewable transition, refineries, biofuels
4. **Water security:** Dams, irrigation systems, sea walls
5. **Downstreaming:** Special economic zones, industrial estates
6. **Housing:** National housing programs, settlements

### Nusantara Development (New Capital)
- **Status:** Capital relocation from Jakarta to East Kalimantan
- **Current investment:** Rp65 trillion (private) + Rp89 trillion (state budget through 2024)
- **Target:** Ready as political capital by 2028
- **2025-2029 funding requirement:** Rp407.6 trillion
  - State budget allocation: Rp48.8 trillion
  - Private sector: Rp358.8 trillion needed

**Objectives:**
- 100% renewable electricity
- Net zero by 2045
- Smart city model for Indonesia

## Trade & Foreign Relations

### Recent Trade Developments

**US Tariff Negotiations (2025):**
- **Initial threat:** 32% tariff on Indonesian imports (April 2025)
- **Negotiated rate:** Reduced to 19% (July 15, 2025)
- **Effective date:** August 1, 2025
- **Trade pact:** Indonesian businesses signed $34B trade & investment agreement with US counterparts (July 2025)

**EU Trade Agreement:**
- **Political agreement:** Reached July 13, 2025 (after 9 years of negotiations)
- **Type:** Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA)
- **Purpose:** Promote trade, investment, inclusive growth, supply chain resilience

- **Exports:** $261.8 billion (+1.6% from 2023)
- **Imports:** $233.8 billion (+5.2% from 2023)
- **Trade surplus:** $28.1 billion (-20.7% from 2023)
- **Balance:** Strong demand domestically; export prices supported by commodity prices

**Key Export Components:**
- Non-oil & gas exports: Primary driver
- Coal, palm oil, metalliferous ores: Major commodities
- Manufacturing products: Growing importance

### International Organization Memberships
- **BRICS (January 6, 2025):** Became full member
- **OECD:** Accession candidate since February 2024; submitted memorandum June 3, 2025
- **ASEAN:** Active member with rotating chairmanship
- **G20:** Previous chair (2021-2022); continues engagement
- **WTO:** Founding member; ratified Trade Facilitation Agreement (2017)

### Foreign Investment
**2024 Performance:**
- Total FDI: $24.7 billion (preliminary)
- Major sources: Singapore, China (HK), South Korea
- Key sectors: Manufacturing, transportation, real estate

**Portfolio Investment (2024):** $12.2 billion (net inflow)

**Scope Expansion:** Extended from mining (nickel, copper, tin) to 2+ dozen commodities

**Priority Commodities:**
- Mining: Nickel, tin, copper, bauxite, iron/steel, silica sand
- Energy: Oil, gas, petroleum

## Key Risks & Challenges

### Global External Risks
1. **US Trade Policy:** Tariff threats (though negotiated to 19% as of July 2025)
2. **Global Financial Uncertainty:** Developed country monetary policy spillovers
3. **Commodity Price Volatility:** Export dependence on oil, coal, palm oil, metals
4. **Capital Flow Reversals:** Risk of sudden outflows from emerging markets

### Domestic Risks
1. **Natural Disasters:** Earthquakes, tsunamis, floods (Indonesian geology)
2. **Environmental Pressures:** Deforestation, land subsidence, water scarcity
3. **Inflation Control:** Food price shocks remain primary risk
4. **Regional Inequality:** Java-centric economic activity
5. **Employment:** Youth unemployment persistence

### Fiscal Challenges
1. **Budget Deficit Control:** Target <3% of GDP (2025: -0.4%)
2. **Debt-to-GDP:** Current 39.9% (below 60% limit); need fiscal consolidation
3. **Subsidy Burden:** Energy subsidies (Rp203.4T in 2025)

### Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA) with European Union

**Date Concluded:** September 23, 2025

**Trade Liberalization:**
- Eliminate tariffs on 98%+ of tariff lines
- Liberalize nearly 100% of trade value over transition period
- Modern, comprehensive rules covering goods, services, and investment

**Coverage:**
- Digital trade and e-commerce
- Government procurement modernization
- Intellectual property protections
- Subsidies and state-owned enterprises regulation
- Trade and sustainable development standards
- Binding dispute settlement with institutional cooperation

**Investment Protection Agreement (IPA):**
- Modern investor protections aligned with international standards
- Safeguards against unlawful expropriation
- Non-discrimination and fair & equitable treatment
- Preserves right to regulate for public policy objectives
- Strengthens investment climate and market certainty

### Policy Coordination: Bank Indonesia & Ministry of Finance (September 4, 2025)

**Interest Cost Sharing Mechanism:**
- Effective 2025, continuing for agreed period
- Applies to Government affordable housing programs
- Extends to Koperasi Desa Merah Putih (Village Red-White Cooperatives)

**How It Works:**
- Budget allocations placed with government agencies and financial institutions
- Returns on placements offset interest costs
- Remaining interest costs split 50/50 between Ministry of Finance and Bank Indonesia
- BI contribution: Additional interest credited to government accounts at BI
- Compliant with Bank Indonesia Act State Treasury function
- Maintains market discipline and financial system integrity

**Bank Indonesia Securities Purchases:**
- Resumed SBN (Government Securities) purchases in secondary market
- Purchases as of September 16, 2025: Rp217.1 trillion
- Includes debt-switching operations
- Reflects expansionary monetary policy stance

## Conclusion

Indonesia is at a critical juncture of development (January 2026). The new Prabowo administration's "Asta Cita" strategy represents an ambitious pivot toward inclusive growth, self-reliance, and infrastructure-driven transformation. With moderate inflation, healthy foreign reserves, and positive investor sentiment, the country is positioned for sustainable growth—provided it can navigate global uncertainties, manage its fiscal consolidation goals, and maintain policy coordination across multiple government levels.
